From Humble Beginnings to Educational Excellence

Cisira Combined Primary School began with a vision to provide quality education to the children of Peddie. What started as a small community initiative has grown into a respected educational institution.

Today, we continue our legacy of academic excellence, character development, and community service, preparing students for success in secondary education and beyond.

Our Mission & Vision

Guiding principles that shape our educational approach and community values

Our Commitment to Balanced Education

We believe every child deserves access to quality education that develops both academic skills and strong character. Our mission is threefold:

  • Academic Excellence: Provide comprehensive education meeting national standards
  • Character Development: Instill morality, discipline, and respect for all
  • Community Preparation: Prepare students for secondary education and life beyond

Our Vision

To provide learners with knowledge, skills, attitude, and values needed in life and prepare them for secondary education, ensuring what they experience from us will benefit them in future.

Policy Aims

Core

This policy aims to establish clear and consistent expectations for all members of our school community:

Establish a disciplined environment conducive to effective education and growth.
Protect the rights and safety of all learners and staff.
Ensure learners uphold the school's reputation with pride and positive conduct.
Promote respect for school property, educators, and fellow learners.

Rights & Responsibilities

Essential

Learner Rights

Right to learn without disturbance in a peaceful environment.
Right to personal safety and dignity, free from violence.
Right to a clean, well-maintained school environment.
Right to a fair and unbiased hearing regarding disciplinary matters.

Learner Responsibilities

Responsibility for regular and punctual school attendance.
Responsibility to care diligently for all school property and resources.
Responsibility to respect all educators and follow school rules consistently.
Responsibility to respect fellow learners and promote cooperation.

School Attendance

Regular and punctual attendance is mandatory for all students.
All absences must be substantiated with a written explanation from a parent/guardian.
Students feeling unwell during the school day must report to the school office immediately.
Administrative permission is required to leave the school premises during school hours.
Non-emergency medical appointments should, where possible, be scheduled outside of school time.

Grooming & Uniform

Personal Grooming

Nails must be kept clean, neat, and appropriate length.
Only plain watches and essential medical bracelets are permitted (No jewelry).
Cosmetics (e.g., makeup) are strictly prohibited during school hours.

Hair Policy

Boys: Hair must be short, neat, and without patterns or fashion cuts.
Girls: Hair must be neatly tied back, plaited, or braided.
Only school-appropriate colors (e.g., black, navy) for hair accessories are allowed.
Strictly Official School Uniform is Mandatory

The uniform must be worn correctly at all times and maintained in a clean, presentable condition.

Serious Misconduct

Zero Tolerance

All acts of serious misconduct will be dealt with severely by the Principal, Disciplinary Committee, and School Governing Body, resulting in formal disciplinary action.

Examples of Zero-Tolerance Offences include:

Possession, use, or distribution of dangerous weapons, alcohol, or illegal substances.
Any form of physical or verbal assault, fighting, or bullying.
Theft, fraud, or intentional vandalism of school or private property.
Racism, sexism, or any other form of malicious discrimination or intimidation.
Gross indecency or inappropriate public behavior.

Frequently Asked Questions

Our school operates from 7:30 AM to 2:30 PM, Monday through Friday. We follow the Eastern Cape Department of Education academic calendar with four terms per year.

We maintain high standards of discipline focused on positive reinforcement and character development. Our discipline policy emphasizes respect, responsibility, and restoration.

We encourage parent involvement through our Parent-Teacher Association, school events, and volunteer opportunities. Regular parent-teacher meetings are scheduled throughout the year.

We provide individualized support for students with learning difficulties through our dedicated teaching staff, including differentiated instruction and additional learning support.
